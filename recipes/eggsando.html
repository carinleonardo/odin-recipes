Egg Sando

Description

One of my go to meals is an egg (tagmago) sando. I love the combination of soft, fluffy bread with a simple, savory spread. What makes an egg sando unique is it's use of Shokupan or "milk bread", a sweet and pillowy, Japanese bread.

For this recipe, I used Just One Cookbook's Japanese Egg Sandwich recipe

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 4 slices shokupan
  • salted butter
  • 1/4 tsp. sugar
  • 1/4 tsp. kosher salt
  • 2 tsp milk
  • 2 tbsp Japanese mayonnaise

Steps

Preparing the Egg Salad

  1. Put the eggs in a medium saucepan filled with water. Bring it to a boil and cook for 12 minutes.
  2. Once finished, transfer the eggs into a bowl of iced water to stop the cooking process. Peel the eggshells.
  3. Transfer the peeled eggs into a bowl and mash with a fork.
  4. Add sugar, salt, milk, and mayonnaise.

Assembling the Egg Sando

  1. Lay out two slices of bread. Butter one side of each slice of bread. Generously spread the egg salad on on slice of bread.
  2. Cut the crust off the bread.
  3. Cut the sandwich in half and serve.
