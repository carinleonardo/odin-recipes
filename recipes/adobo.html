Adobo Fried Rice

Description

Adobo is a staple in our home. My mom always cooks a large batch because it stores well and, arguably, tastes better the longer it sits in the fridge.

It's easy to get tired of eating the same thing every day. One of the best ways to spruce up leftover adobo is by turning it into Adobo Fried Rice.

For this recipe, I reference Manila Spoon.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp neutral Oil, vegetable, canola, avocado
  • 5 cloves Garlic, minced
  • Leftover, cooked rice
  • Left-over Pork Adobo, shredded
  • Salt, to taste
  • Green onions
  • Soy sauce, for color
  • Fried egg

Steps

  1. Head the oil in a woke or large frying pan on meadium heat. Cook the garlic until golden brown. Add the shredded pork adobo and include any leftover sauce.
  2. Add the prepared rice into the garlic and rice mixture. Stir-fry until rice is heated. Season with salt to taste and add soy sauce deepen the fried rice's color.
  3. Garnish with sliced green onions and top with a fried egg.
