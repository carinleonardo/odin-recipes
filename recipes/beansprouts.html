Kongnamul-muchim

Soybean Sprout side dish

Description

Having multiple side dishes on a table keeps a meal interesting. Kongnamul-muchim is one of my favorite Korean side dishes because it is fresh, umami, and easy-to-make! I follow Maangchi's recipe, but substitute fish sauce with dashi powder.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. soybean sprouts, rinsed and drained
  • 1 tsp. kosher salt
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp. of Korean hot pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 2 tsp dashi powder
  • 2 tsp roasted sesame seeds
  • 2 tsp roasted sesame oil

Steps

  1. Place soybean sprouts in a pot. Add the salt and Â½ cup of water and cover. Bring to a boil over medium high heat for 10 minutes. Drain.
  2. Put the cooked soybean sprouts in a mixing bowl. Add garlic, hot pepper flakes, green onion, dashi, sesame seeds, and toasted sesame oil and mix by hand.
  3. Transfer to a serving plate and serve.
